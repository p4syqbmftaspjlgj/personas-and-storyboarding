OBSERVATIONS & INTERVIEWS

People spent max 1-2 minutes at the station, and the time spent lingering was usually on the “retreive phone” page where it asks the user to input their pin to unlock the box with their phone

When people chose a box for their phone, they almost always chose the first available box highest from the ground. In other words, the red boxes were clustered at the top while the blue, available boxes were toward the bottom

Within an hour-long window, most few people did not retrieve their phone, indicating people spent a long time with their phones away and charged

Questions

1) Why are you using this charging station?

2) How often do you charge your phone here?

3) Do you come here out of necessity or because you want to?

4) When do you most often use this charging station?

5) How long do you charge your phone for?

6) Do you make decisions on where to study based on the availability of charging stations such as these?

User 1 Response Summary

- This person said they used the charging station charge their phone and keep it out of sight while they studied

- They viewed it as "killing two birds with one stone" -- they got to take a break from distractions while charging their device

- User 1 said they use the charging station every time they come to the SciLi, which is several times a week

- They charge their phone for about an hour or so before getting up to check their phone and keep studying

- They don't come to the SciLi just for the station, they view it as an added bonus

User 2 Response Summary

- User 2's phone was dead, and they came to the SciLi for the sole purpose of charging their phone

- They had to contact some friends and only had their phone, which was out of battery. Didn't have their laptop

- Probably uses the station a few times a semester, generally when they don't have a charger handy

- They charge their phone for about 20 minutes, however long it takes to get initial charge

- They only come to SciLi basmement for the charging station. They don't choose to study here for the station

User 3 Response Summary

- User 3 (a first year) said it was their first time using the charging station

- Their phone died while studying, they didn't have a charger so their friend reccomended they'd try it

- Now that they know of the charging station's availability, they are more inclined to use it more often. They view it as a safety precaustion, too

- They charged their phone for about two hours total, checking their phone about an hour in

- They tend to study at SciLi basement often and will likely use it again
